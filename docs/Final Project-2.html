Introduction:

Thousands of people every year scour the internet reding reviews about what car to buy. Usually a lengthy and time comsuming process because different brands and their makes and models will be on different pages. Many basic obervations can be made based on the amount of stars given and the "top reviews" or what the website deems "most useful reviews". This tutorial is to provide more in depth observations into what people are actually saying about each of these brands.

About the Data:

The first step to data analysis is to choose and obtain a dataset. I chose Consumer Car Ratings and Reviews from Edmunds. Edmunds offers car reviews and insights into vehicle rankings, road tests, best of vehicle lists, editorial recommendations, etc., as well as provides values & pricing information. Edmunds is a leading website for consumers looking to buy a used or new vehicle, sell their vehicle, or estimate the costs associated with a vehicle. From comprehensive reviews and vehicle rankings to car-buying & valuation tools, Edmunds is an one-stop resource for consumers and dealership owners to ensure fair pricing and deals. Although Edumunds reviews are provided to the public in a useable format, Kaggle, a free website that contains datasets for community use, has publised csv files containing all the compiled reviews for each make and model for 62 car brands. Unfortunately, Most car brands had pretty high reviews so it was hard to pick a distinct front runner. However, this does not mean that there isn't other ways to determine this. With more factors in the equation such as period of ownership, making a more certain recommendation could come easier.
